Lets Get Travelling!

Antigua

  1. Shirley Heights, an old military base for the 54th Regiment (2nd Battalion Dorset) from 1840 to the 1850s.
  2. Feeling adventurous? Take a pair of long trousers and boots and walk through the bushes to find undisturbed ruins. Otherwise head to the lookout point and grab a very strong rum punch while looking out over English Harbour, you are 490ft above sea level.
  3. Betty’s Hope, the only sugar mill on the island, which has been restored with a museum attached. The work that has gone into excavating the mill and surrounding plantation grounds is fantastic. The museum captures the basic history of colonialism, slavery and the impact on Antigua.
  4. With a rented car, or taxi, take a ride down fig tree drive (the best time is in the summer months when the trees are in bloom). Along the way you should see Antigua Blacks, the islands pineapple, growing in the old plantation grounds.
  5. St. Johns, Antigua’s city centre is a wonderful place to explore but to get a real feel for the island stay away from the port end of town as it is full of touristy stalls. Instead, either discover the old Cathedral (Church Street), Antiguan museum (Long Street) or the market where you get caught up in the hustle and bustle of daily Antiguan life.
  6. Visiting Hemmingway’s, located on St. Mary’s Street, with balcony seats overlook the busy street below which makes for a great break from the heat of town.
  7. Fancy staying in the water and exploring the exotic array of marine life? Then head to Jolly Dive.
  8. You can also go Swimming with Sting-rays. The company take you out in a group of boats to a nearby reef. You are able to swim in the middle of the ocean surrounded by sting-rays, they swim up to you allowing you to ‘hold them’, stroke them and have photos. A surreal experience at most!
  9. Mermaid Gardens, hike through the bush around Gallon Bay to discover the natural pools of water and incredible clift-breaks from the crashing water.
  10. There are 365 beaches in Antigua – what else is there to say?

Havanna, Cuba

  1. Walk down the Paseo de Marti Boulevard to watch the people of Cuba relax after work and school.
  2. View the historic communist statue throughout the city, including the Estudiantes de Medicina and Maxime Gomez.
  3. Visit the Capital Building.
  4. Gran Teatro de La Habana, built in 1838 the building is home to the Cuban national Ballet and the entrance hall was astounding.
  5. Museum of the Revolution. Located in what was the Presidential Palace the rooms have been well preserved. Inside covers the history of the Cuban revolution and the following years under Castro. Full of photos, memorials and newspaper articles used during the revolution.
  6. Rent a classic Cuban car to drive along the Malecon to the Hotel Nacional de Cuba. The luxury hotel was declared a National Monument, standing for 85 years, and part of the World Heritage Site. While at the hotel visit the famous cocktail bar, walk through the gardens and hotel to see the old theatre decked out in rich reds and dark wood.
  7. Explore New Havana and see the famous Plaza de la Revolucion, which is surrounded by government buildings, which displayed memorials, including the famous one of Che Guevara.
  8. Day trip to Parquet Historico Militae Morro-cabana: Located across the water from Habana Vieja.
  9. The Partagas Cigar Factory.
  10. The Old Havana police station, a castle/fortress. While there visit the Plaza da Armas, the oldest square in Havana and the first castle built in Havana. The square is lined with royal palms and hosts a daily second hand book market (excluding Sunday) and at the centre is the statue of Carlos Manuel De Cespedes, who is perceived as leading the campaign of independence for Cuba in 1868.

Rent a little party boat in Mallorca?

Em here - fancy a short trip sailing around Mallorca and Ibiza etc? Thought it might be fun to see these sites. May 2019!

  1. Formentor Beach. Located on the Island of Illa de Formentor with perfectly clear water.
  2. Porto Cristo. With great marinas and the underground cave system of lakes, Caves of Drach. Adults 14.50 euro for a guided tour with a classical quartet and boat ride on Lake Martel.
  3. Cala Santenyi. Southeast of Mallorca. The famous ‘es pontas’ stone arch is found here. The rock has been eroded by the water over years causing a large hole to be formed in the rock which has been labelled ‘the bridge’ by locals.
  4. Cala Boquer. Only accessible by boat, of foot, this secluded beach looks perfect for peaceful snorkelling.
  5. Resort of Sant Elm which has numerous restaurants overlooking the Natural Parl of Sa Dragonera.
  6. Palma Cathedral and fortress in Mallorca. (Historic site)
  7. Llotja Mallorca (Historic site)
  8. Iglesia de Sant Bartomeu.
